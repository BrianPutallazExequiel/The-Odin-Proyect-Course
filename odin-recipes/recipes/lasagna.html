Lasagna

Description:

Lasagna is a classic Italian dish that embodies layers of flavor and comfort. It starts with wide, flat pasta sheets that are cooked to perfection. Between these layers of pasta, you'll find a rich and savory filling, often consisting of seasoned ground meat, such as beef or pork, combined with a luscious tomato sauce, aromatic herbs, and spices. Interspersed with the meaty mixture are creamy and indulgent layers of béchamel sauce and grated Parmesan or mozzarella cheese. This delightful combination of textures and flavors is then baked to a golden, bubbling perfection in the oven.The result is a mouthwatering masterpiece that offers a harmonious blend of tender pasta, savory meat, tangy tomato sauce, and cheesy goodness. Lasagna is often garnished with fresh basil leaves or more cheese on top for an extra layer of flavor. Whether homemade or enjoyed at an Italian restaurant, lasagna is a beloved comfort food that warms the heart and satisfies the palate.

Ingredients:

  • 500 grams of lasagna noodles
  • 1 pound of ground beef
  • 1 chopped onion
  • 2 cloves of minced garlic
  • 1 can (28 ounces) of tomato sauce
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon of pepper
  • 1 teaspoon of dried oregano
  • 1 cup of ricotta cheese
  • 2 cups of shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/2 cup of grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 egg
  • 2 tablespoons of fresh chopped parsley
  • Olive oil

Steps:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Boil the lasagna noodles according to the package instructions until they are al dente. Drain and set aside.
  3. In a large skillet, heat some olive oil over medium-high heat. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic, and sauté until they become fragrant and translucent.
  4. Add the ground beef to the skillet and cook until it's browned and fully cooked. Drain any excess fat.
  5. Stir in the tomato sauce, salt, pepper, and dried oregano. Simmer the sauce for about 10 minutes.
  6. In a separate bowl, combine the ricotta cheese, 1 cup of mozzarella cheese, grated Parmesan cheese, egg, and chopped parsley.
  7. Spread a thin layer of the meat sauce in the bottom of a baking dish. Place a layer of lasagna noodles on top.
  8. Spread half of the cheese mixture over the noodles, then add another layer of meat sauce. Repeat the layers.
  9. Sprinkle the remaining mozzarella cheese on top.
  10. Cover the baking dish with aluminum foil and bake for 25 minutes. Then, remove the foil and bake for an additional 25 minutes, or until the lasagna is bubbly and the cheese is golden brown.
  11. Let the lasagna cool for a few minutes before serving. Enjoy!
